MATAKA STATION

Land and Sea

Joined in Harmony

From the sun-drenched Pacific coast, leading east to the rugged tip of Purerua Peninsula, and wrapping west into calm Bay of Islands waters, Mataka Station is equal parts stunning coastline and majestic interior. Our varied shores offer a launch-point for adventure and a sanctuary for serenity. They embrace our vast interior, with its commanding mountains, forested valleys, dramatic cliffs, and expansive farmland. Walking and riding trails weave through this oasis of tranquility–visiting springs, streams, lakes, and waterfalls along the way. Elegant homes are dotted around the coast, for people who share a dream of living immersed in nature and surrounded by the sea.

How It Works

Privacy and Community

Mataka Station comprises just 30 lots of 20–57 hectares (50 - 140 acres). All lot owners belong to the Mataka Residents Association, an Incorporated Society governed by a constitution and a committee of elected officers. Membership grants shared access to all our roads, beaches, and trails, and the beach lodge, boatsheds, and mountaintop. Owners can build a home on a designated site within their lot. An independent panel ensures each home aligns with Mataka’s design guidelines. Owners may fence up to four hectares privately. They lease the remainder to the Association for conservation and farming. Each owner’s annual levy (about NZ$40,000) supports management of the infrastructure, leased land, and shared facilities.

Our Beaches

Your Choice

Mataka Beach is absolute perfection. Hundreds of meters of fine sand and dazzling sun front the Pacific Ocean, bookended by rock pools, cliffs, a waterfall, and the shade of enormous ancient pohutukawa trees. Further east, Nine Pin Track brings you to Nine Pin Beach and its notoriously abundant fishing. Our Bay of Islands shoreline offers the incredible seclusion of Howe Beach, and our boating-oriented beach and facilities at Whale Bay.

The Beach Lodge

The Crown Jewel

Sitting demurely back from the sheer magnificence of Mataka Beach, the Beach Lodge is home base for bliss-filled days of relaxation and fun with friends and family. A masterpiece of architectural form, its simplicity conceals a wealth of function. It’s fully equipped to address every want and need—indoor and outdoor cooking, dining and drinking, cosy fireside seating, and showers and toilets. Everything you need is in place, from beach towels and candles to pots and pans.

The Boatsheds at Whale Bay

Form Meets Function

The boatsheds at Whale Bay are designed for both practicality and beauty, offering residents a seamless connection to the water. With ample storage for boating equipment and direct access to the bay, they support a lifestyle of adventure and leisure, perfectly integrated with Mataka Station’s coastal charm.

Mount Mataka

The View from On High

At 258 meters, Mount Mataka offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean, Bay of Islands, and the rolling landscapes of the station. Accessible via scenic trails, it’s a haven for reflection and a testament to the natural majesty that defines Mataka Station.

The Trail Network

An Intimate Journey Through Nature

Mataka Station’s 30 kilometers of trails wind through native bush, past streams, lakes, and waterfalls. Perfect for walking, riding, or simply soaking in the tranquility, the network connects residents to the heart of the landscape, fostering a deep connection with nature.

The Farm

Prize Breeds of Sheep and Cattle

The working farm at Mataka Station sustains 1600 sheep and 200 cattle, managed sustainably to preserve the land’s natural beauty. Residents can engage with this agricultural heritage, enjoying the fruits of a landscape that balances productivity with conservation.
